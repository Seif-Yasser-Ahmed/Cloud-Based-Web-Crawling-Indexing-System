Traditional data sourcing is leaving companies behind the competition


When companies need specific data from particular sources, they often commission custom-coded feeds, which come with two primary obstacles: high upfront costs and delayed deployment. Each new website adds an expensive setup fee, creating a financial barrier that slows progress and limits growth potential.


This setup leads to:


  • Speed limitations that prevent timely market insights and hinder data-driven decision-making.

  • Cost barriers, which reduce the ability to scale. Companies are forced to limit the number of sites they can draw from, compromising the breadth of their insights.


Faced with these challenges, many businesses struggle to achieve full-spectrum visibility from the web data they need. The question remains: How can companies source data from more sites at scale without escalating their costs?

Current solutions are still limited


Companies currently have three main approaches to web data collection, each with its strengths and drawbacks:


  1. Pre-scraped data vendors provide quick, cost-effective access to data, eliminating the need for setup. However, these vendors generally lack customization and focus on popular sites with fixed data points, making it challenging to gather niche data.

  2. Custom feed services deliver fresh, on-demand data with quality assurance, allowing companies to extract data from any site or data point. While these services offer flexibility, they require significant setup time, incur upfront costs, and add a per-site fee, making scaling economically challenging.

  3. In-house scraping solutions give companies full control and provide customization options similar to custom feed services. However, they involve high maintenance costs. Managing anti-ban measures, compliance, and specialized labor increases expenses and operational complexity, limiting speed and scalability.


The ideal solution would enable businesses to quickly and affordably acquire high-quality, customized data feeds from any site without high setup fees or operational overhead.
